About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Scientist in the Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science at Columbia University, affiliated with both the Applied Physics and Applied Mathematics Department and the Data Science Institute. My general research interests lie in nonlinear PDEs, Approximation Theory and Machine Learning.

I am working with Qiang Du on the mathematical analysis of Deep Learning models, and on numerical algorithms for nonlocal PDEs with applications in peridynamics. Recently, I have also been approached by the Shvartsman group at Princeton University to collaborate on a project about pattern formation on the sphere with applications in the dynamics of cells and tissues.

I received my PhD in Applied Mathematics in 2017 from the University of Oxford where I studied numerical algorithms for differential equations with Nick Trefethen. Prior to this I completed in 2013 a M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics at the University of Toulouse and a M.Sc. in Aerospace Engineering at the French National Higher School of Aeronautics (where I worked with Marc Montagnac and Xavier Claeys). In 2011, I spent six months at the University of Manchester doing research in Astrophysics with Clive Dickinson.
