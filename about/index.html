About

I am a Chu Assistant Professor of Applied Mathematics in the Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science at Columbia University. My general research interests lie in nonlinear PDEs, approximation theory and machine learning.

I am working with Qiang Du on the mathematical analysis of deep learning models, and with Qiang and Mikael Slevinsky on numerical algorithms for nonlocal PDEs with applications in peridynamics.

I am also collaborating with the Shvartsman group at Princeton University and Eric Vanden-Eijnden at the Courant Institute on a couple of projects about pattern formation on the sphere with applications in biophysics.

I received my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from the University of Oxford in 2017, where I studied numerical algorithms for differential equations with Nick Trefethen. Prior to this I completed in 2013 a M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics at the University of Toulouse, as well as a M.Sc. in Aerospace Engineering at the French National Higher School of Aeronautics where I worked with Marc Montagnac and Xavier Claeys. In 2011, I spent six months at the University of Manchester doing research in Astrophysics with Clive Dickinson.
